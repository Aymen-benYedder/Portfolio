## Executive Summary

Canada's International Mobility Program (IMP) offers several LMIA-exempt work permit pathways that are strategically critical for global tech talent. The **Intra-Company Transfer (ICT)** — under exemption codes **C61/C62/C63** (post-2022 renumbering, formerly C12) — allows multinational enterprises to transfer executives, senior managers, and specialized knowledge workers to Canadian affiliates without a labour market test. It requires ≥1 year continuous employment with the foreign entity in the preceding 3 years and a qualifying corporate relationship (parent, subsidiary, branch, or affiliate).

The **C10 Significant Benefit** category (R205(a), officer discretion) is a catch-all for foreign nationals whose work brings "significant economic, social, or cultural benefit" to Canada. **As of February 24, 2026, IRCC tightened C10 significantly** — officers are now instructed to reserve it for "unique or exceptional situations" only, with quantifiable evidence of broader community/regional impact beyond the employer.

ICT remains the more structured, predictable pathway for tech workers at multinationals. C10 is now a high-scrutiny, evidence-intensive route reserved for exceptional individual contributors, researchers, or entrepreneurs who demonstrably transcend a standard hire.

The **Start-Up Visa program is paused** as of January 1, 2026 (closing to new commitment certificates Dec 31, 2025; final applications with valid 2025 certificates accepted until June 30, 2026). A replacement entrepreneur pilot is expected in 2026. The **Self-Employed Persons Program** remains paused since April 2024.

**Key stat:** IMP LMIA-exempt permits target 170,000 in 2026 (+32% YoY), while TFWP LMIA-required targets dropped to 60,000 — a structural shift toward exemptions.

## ICT Program Details

### Legal Basis & Exemption Codes

- **Regulation:** R205(a) of IRPR — "significant economic, social, or cultural benefit"
- **Current codes (since Dec 15, 2022 renumbering):**
  - **C61** — ICT Start-up (new Canadian branch/subsidiary/affiliate)
  - **C62** — ICT Executive / Senior Manager / Functional Manager (existing entity)
  - **C63** — ICT Specialized Knowledge (existing entity)
- **Legacy code C12** was retired in 2024; older permits may still show C12

### Three Qualifying Role Categories

| Category | Definition | TEER/NOC | Max Stay |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Executive** | Directs the organization or major component; establishes goals/policies; broad discretionary decision-making | TEER 0 | 7 years |
| **Senior / Functional Manager** | Manages organization, function, or department; supervises other managers/professionals; day-to-day operational authority | TEER 0/1 | 7 years |
| **Specialized Knowledge** | Possesses advanced proprietary knowledge of the company's product/service/process AND advanced expertise requiring extensive experience/education | TEER 0/1 | 5 years |

### Core Eligibility Requirements

1. **Qualifying corporate relationship** — Foreign & Canadian entities must be parent-subsidiary, branch, or affiliate (common ownership/control). Joint ventures and contractual relationships do **not** qualify.
2. **Multinational status** — The foreign enterprise must be an existing multinational corporation with revenue-generating operations in **at least two countries** (per 2025/2026 Fragomen-reported guidance update). Companies operating in only one country cannot use ICT to establish a first foreign branch.
3. **1-year tenure rule** — Employee must have been employed continuously, full-time by the foreign entity for **≥1 year within the 3 years preceding the application**, in a capacity comparable to the Canadian role.
4. **Comparable role** — The Canadian position must be at the same **or higher** functional level as the foreign role. Cannot transfer a specialized knowledge worker into a generalist role.
5. **Active business** — Both entities must be actively, regularly, and continuously doing business (not shell companies). Virtual offices/home offices/P.O. boxes → immediate refusal.
6. **Genuine Canadian business purpose** — Must demonstrate a real operational need, not a paper transfer.

### Specialized Knowledge Trap — 2026 Scrutiny

- IRCC officers heavily scrutinize "specialized knowledge" claims. Simply knowing the company's internal software is **insufficient**.
- Knowledge must be **both** proprietary (unique to the company) **and** advanced (not widely available in the Canadian labour market).
- 2025/2026 guidance: applicants should have **≥2 years** experience with the foreign enterprise. If less (but >1 year), must demonstrate studies in the relevant field or industry experience — subject to higher scrutiny.
- Must demonstrate that training a Canadian hire would cause significant business disruption.
- Prevailing Canadian wage must be met or exceeded for the occupation.

### Duration Limits

| Scenario | Initial Permit | Max Cumulative |
|---|---|---|
| Established entity — Executive/Manager | Up to 3 years | 7 years |
| Established entity — Specialized Knowledge | Up to 3 years | 5 years |
| New Office / Start-up ICT | Up to 1 year | 1 year initially; extensions possible if active business demonstrated |

**Cooling-off period:** Once maximum stay is reached, the worker must spend **≥1 year outside Canada** working for the company before a new ICT can be issued.

### Processing Times (2026)

| Country / Route | Approximate Time |
|---|---|
| United States | 3–5 weeks |
| United Kingdom | 4–7 weeks |
| UAE | 5–9 weeks |
| India | 6–10 weeks |
| Turkey | 5–8 weeks |
| Online outside Canada (general) | 4–12 weeks (visa-office dependent) |
| Port of Entry (visa-exempt nationals) | Same day (if fully documented) |
| Extensions inside Canada | 8–16 weeks |
| **Global Skills Strategy (GSS) eligible** | **2-week target** (if TEER 0/1 role) |

**IRCC processing times updated May 26, 2026** — trend is upward. Real-world: countries with enhanced background checks (Nigeria, Pakistan, parts of Middle East) see 16–24 weeks.

### Fees

- Employer compliance fee: **CAD $230** (paid via Employer Portal)
- Work permit processing fee: **CAD $155**
- Biometrics fee: **CAD $85** (if applicable)
- Open work permit holder fee (if applicable): **CAD $100**

## C10 Significant Benefit

### Legal Basis

- **Regulation:** R205(a) — "General exemption" / officer discretion
- **Exemption Code:** C10
- **GCMS Case Type:** 52
- **Nature:** Catch-all LMIA exemption when no other code fits, but the work brings quantifiable benefit to Canada

### Who It's For

- Researchers and academics at Canadian institutions
- Highly recognized cultural figures, artists, performers for major Canadian events
- Technology founders relocating startup operations to Canada
- Scarce-skill professionals in STEM, finance, law, medicine (with demonstrable broader impact)
- Workers preventing disruption at major Canadian projects or events
- Workers creating/maintaining significant Canadian employment or training opportunities
- Specialized medical professionals in underserved regions
- Workers supporting technological development or product/service innovation in key Canadian industries

### February 24, 2026 — Major Tightening

| Criterion | Pre-2026 (Previous Instructions) | Post-Feb 24, 2026 (New Instructions) |
|---|---|---|
| **Application type** | Broadly applied to highly skilled or specialized workers | Reserved strictly for **"unique or exceptional situations"** |
| **Scope of benefit** | Benefit to the specific company or immediate project | Must lead to **positive effects on broader community, region, or country** — beyond the applicant, their dependents, and employer |
| **Job creation / training** | Simply "creating training opportunities" was often sufficient | Must create a **"large number"** of employment or training opportunities with **"demonstrable impact"** |
| **Evidence standard** | General claims could suffice | Benefits must be **"valid, reasonable and demonstrable"** — concrete, quantifiable evidence required |
| **Risk assessment** | Not routinely assessed | Officers must weigh potential benefits **against risks** to the Canadian labour market |
| **Officer scrutiny** | Moderate | **Extensive scrutiny** expected on all general exemption C10 applications |

### The "Relative Scale" Clause

The updated guidance clarifies: **"The scale of 'significant' is relative to the industry/town/sector."** A case does not need to be Canada-wide or "top of the top" to qualify. Example: creating 5 jobs might not be "large" in downtown Toronto but could be "significant benefit" in a small rural town.

### Pre-Identified Exemptions (NOT affected by C10 tightening)

These specific scenarios under R205(a) bypass the new scrutiny:
- Marine workers
- Airline personnel
- Rail grinder operators
- Foreign physicians in underserved Quebec regions

### C10 vs C11 — Critical Distinction

| Aspect | C10 | C11 |
|---|---|---|
| **Purpose** | Significant benefit — general (employee or unique contribution) | Entrepreneur / Self-employed / Owner-operator |
| **Who** | Foreign nationals whose work brings broad benefit | Self-employed individuals operating a Canadian business they own/control |
| **Officer discretion** | Very high | Very high |
| **Refusal rates** | Not published; estimated 20-35% at some visa offices | 20-35% at some visa offices (Mumbai, Beijing) |
| **Key evidence** | Quantifiable community/regional impact | Business ownership, investment, job creation |

### C10 Processing Times

- Not separately published by IRCC; falls under IMP work permit general processing
- Online outside Canada: **8–16 weeks** (typical)
- GSS-eligible TEER 0/1 roles: **2-week target** (if employer submits via GSS)
- Real-world: expect **12–20+ weeks** for complex C10 files under new scrutiny regime

## Application Process

### Step-by-Step for ICT (C61/C62/C63)

1. **Corporate Structure Validation** — Confirm qualifying relationship (org charts, share registers, audited financials). Canadian entity must be legally incorporated (Federal/Provincial), have a Business Number (BN) from CRA, and physical commercial premises.

2. **Business Plan (New Office C61)** — If establishing a new Canadian office: financial capacity (recommended liquid funds ≥$100,000 CAD for first-year costs), realistic staffing plans, physical commercial lease.

3. **Employer Portal Submission** — Canadian entity submits Offer of Employment via IRCC Employer Portal. Includes: business activities, job duties, wage, LMIA exemption code. Pays **$230 compliance fee**. Obtains Offer of Employment number.

4. **Worker Application** — Foreign national submits work permit application (typically IMM 1295):
   - Online through IRCC, or
   - At Port of Entry (visa-exempt nationals only), or
   - From inside Canada (if already holding valid status)
   - Pay **$155 processing fee** + **$85 biometrics** (if applicable)

5. **Supporting Documents** (see checklist below)

6. **Biometrics and Medical** — If required, applicant attends VAC for biometrics. Medical exam if >6 months stay or from designated country.

7. **Processing & Decision** — IRCC reviews. May issue:
   - Approval letter (present at POE for permit issuance)
   - Request for additional documents
   - Refusal

### Documentation Checklist

**Corporate Documents:**
- [ ] Incorporation certificates (foreign & Canadian entities)
- [ ] Business Number (BN) from CRA for Canadian entity
- [ ] Audited financial statements (last 2-3 years) for both entities
- [ ] Corporate organization charts (with shareholdings showing qualifying relationship)
- [ ] Share register / common ownership proof
- [ ] Commercial lease or property deed for Canadian office
- [ ] Business license(s)
- [ ] Tax returns / T4 summaries
- [ ] Proof of active business operations (payroll records, client contracts, invoices)

**For New Office (C61) — Additional:**
- [ ] Detailed business plan for Canadian entity (3-year projection)
- [ ] Proof of liquid funds ≥$100,000 CAD
- [ ] Staffing plan with hiring timeline
- [ ] Market research for Canadian market entry
- [ ] Photographs of commercial premises (recommended)

**Worker Documents:**
- [ ] Employment contract / offer letter from Canadian entity
- [ ] Employment history letter from foreign entity (confirming ≥1 year continuous, full-time in qualifying role within past 3 years)
- [ ] Pay stubs / T4-equivalents from foreign entity
- [ ] Detailed job description for both foreign and Canadian roles
- [ ] CV / résumé
- [ ] Education credentials (degrees, diplomas)
- [ ] Professional certifications (if applicable)
- [ ] Proof of specialized knowledge (training logs, unique certifications, proprietary methodology documentation, patent filings) — **critical for C63**
- [ ] Passport (valid) + copy of all pages
- [ ] Digital passport photo
- [ ] IMM 1295 application form
- [ ] Family information form (IMM 5645 / IMM 5707)
- [ ] Police certificates (if required)
- [ ] Medical exam results (if required)
- [ ] Proof of language ability (not mandatory for ICT, but strengthens PR pathway)

**C10-Specific Additional Evidence:**
- [ ] Quantifiable benefit projections (job creation numbers, investment amounts, revenue impact)
- [ ] Third-party corroboration (industry letters, news coverage, contract values, government endorsements)
- [ ] Evidence of "uniqueness" — why this individual specifically cannot be replaced by a Canadian hire
- [ ] Broader community/regional impact documentation
- [ ] Risk-mitigation statement (addressing potential labour market concerns)
- [ ] Letters of support from industry associations, municipal/provincial economic development agencies

### Employer Portal Compliance Obligations

- Employer must submit Offer of Employment and pay $230 compliance fee **before** worker applies
- Employer is subject to **compliance reviews** for 6 years after permit issuance
- Records to maintain: payroll, T4s, job duties, work location, employee contact info
- Non-compliance penalties: warnings, administrative monetary penalties (up to $50,000), or bar from IMP for 1-2 years

## Success Rates & Refusal Reasons

### ICT Refusal Rates

ICT generally has higher approval rates than C10/C11 due to its structured criteria. Exact published refusal rates are not available from IRCC by exemption code, but immigration practitioner estimates:

- **C62/C63 (established entity):** Estimated approval rate **75–85%** for well-prepared files
- **C61 (new office / start-up):** Estimated approval rate **60–70%** — higher scrutiny
- **Port of Entry applications:** Higher risk of refusal if documentation is incomplete

### Top Refusal Reasons — ICT

| Rank | Reason | How to Mitigate |
|---|---|---|
| **1** | **Specialized knowledge not genuinely "specialized"** — officer finds knowledge is standard industry knowledge widely available in Canada (esp. for C63) | Document proprietary nature explicitly; provide training logs, unique certifications, evidence knowledge is not commonly available; show business disruption if person is absent |
| **2** | **Corporate relationship not established** — insufficient documentation of parent-subsidiary or affiliate status, especially with complex holding structures | Provide org charts, share registers, audited financials, and a clear narrative of the relationship |
| **3** | **Canadian entity not actively doing business** — recently incorporated with no established operations; officer concludes it's a shell company | Show payroll, client contracts, invoices, commercial lease, business license, active bank account |
| **4** | **Less than 1 year of prior employment** — gaps in continuous employment within the 3-year window | Ensure precise documentation of continuous full-time employment; part-time or gaps cause problems |
| **5** | **Role at Canadian entity is not qualifying category** — actual duties are operational/line-level, not executive/managerial/specialized knowledge | Write detailed job descriptions that clearly match TEER 0/1 categories; avoid generalist language |
| **6** | **Mismatched documentation** — discrepancies between Employer Portal submission and work permit application (duties, location, wage) | Ensure absolute consistency between all submitted documents |
| **7** | **Pay gap / below prevailing wage** — significant underpayment relative to Canadian market rate for the occupation | Pay at or above prevailing Canadian wage for the NOC |
| **8** | **Virtual office / residential address** — IRCC cracked down on non-commercial locations | Secure physical commercial premises; home offices and P.O. boxes cause automatic refusal |
| **9** | **Company not multinational** — foreign enterprise does not have operations in ≥2 countries (2025/2026 guidance) | Cannot use ICT if the foreign company operates in only one country; consider C11 instead |

### Top Refusal Reasons — C10 Significant Benefit

| Rank | Reason |
|---|---|
| **1** | **Benefit not demonstrable** — vague assertions without concrete evidence (job numbers, investment, IP transfer) |
| **2** | **Not "unique or exceptional"** — post-Feb 2026, officer finds the situation is a routine hire |
| **3** | **Benefit limited to employer only** — does not extend to broader community, region, or sector |
| **4** | **Temporary intent not established** — officer suspects applicant intends to stay permanently without proper PR channels |
| **5** | **Adverse immigration history** — prior overstays, refusals, or violations undermine credibility |
| **6** | **Insufficient evidence of "large number" of jobs/training** — general claims don't meet the new higher threshold |

### C10 Refusal Rate Estimates

- Estimated **20–35% refusal rate** at some visa offices (Mumbai, Beijing, historically)
- **Likely higher post-Feb 2026** as new scrutiny guidelines take effect
- Applications from smaller employers or for standard tech roles face highest risk

## PR Pathways from ICT and C10

### ICT → Permanent Residence

ICT work permits are temporary but provide an excellent runway to PR. The skilled Canadian work experience (TEER 0 or 1) is highly valued.

**Primary PR Pathways:**

| Pathway | Key Requirement | Benefit for ICT Holders |
|---|---|---|
| **Canadian Experience Class (CEC)** — Express Entry | ≥12 months skilled (TEER 0/1/2/3) Canadian work experience in last 3 years + language test | ICT work directly counts as Canadian experience; no LMIA needed for job offer after 1 year |
| **Federal Skilled Worker Program (FSWP)** — Express Entry | Skilled work experience (foreign or Canadian) + language + ECA | ICT Canadian experience boosts CRS; foreign experience transfers |
| **Provincial Nominee Programs (PNP)** | Employer-driven stream + job offer in province | Many provinces (ON, BC, AB, SK) target executives/managers; PNP nomination adds **600 CRS points** |
| **Express Entry + PNP** | Nomination from any participating province | Guaranteed Invitation to Apply (ITA); the strongest PR route for ICT holders in 2026 after job offer points removal |

### Critical 2026 Change — Arranged Employment Points REMOVED

- **March 2025 / 2026:** IRCC removed 50-point (TEER 0) and 200-point (TEER 00) arranged employment CRS bonuses from Express Entry
- Previously, ICT holders could claim 50–200 CRS points for a valid job offer after 1 year. **This advantage is gone.**
- **Strategic implication:** ICT holders now need:
  - Higher language scores (target CLB 9+)
  - Spousal language/education improvement
  - French-language ability (for category-based draws at CRS ~393)
  - **PNP nomination** (600 points) — the new clearest path
  - Category-based draws (STEM, healthcare, French, trades)
- **Typical timeline:** ICT → 1 year Canadian experience → Express Entry/PNP → PR in 2–4 years total

### C10 → Permanent Residence

- C10 is a discretionary work permit; it does **not** directly lead to PR
- However, Canadian work experience gained under C10 can qualify for CEC after 12 months
- The same 2026 CRS changes apply — arranged employment points removal affects C10 holders equally
- PNP entrepreneur streams (BC, Alberta, Ontario, Manitoba) allow active C10 permit holders to apply for nomination
- **Strategy:** Plan PR pathway during the C10 work permit, not after

### Start-Up Visa (SUV) — PAUSED in 2026

- **As of January 1, 2026:** SUV program is paused
- **Final deadline:** Valid 2025 commitment certificate holders must apply by **June 30, 2026**
- **Processing time:** 3.3–4.3 years (40–52 months) for queued applications
- **Temporary Work Permit (TWP) bridge:** 2–4 months processing for SUV applicants awaiting PR (also paused for new applicants Dec 19, 2025)
- **Replacement:** A new entrepreneur pilot program is expected in 2026 — details TBA
- **Self-Employed Persons Program:** Paused since April 2024, extended until further notice

### Provincial Entrepreneur Programs (Alternatives to SUV)

| Province | Stream | Min. Investment |
|---|---|---|
| Ontario | OINP Entrepreneur Stream | CAD $200,000–$600,000 |
| British Columbia | BCPNP Entrepreneur Immigration | CAD $200,000+ |

| Manitoba | MPNP Business Investor Stream | CAD $250,000+ |

## Direct Answer: AEO/GEO

**Q: What is the difference between AEO (Arranged Employment Opinion) and GEO (Global Employment Opinion), and how do they relate to LMIA exemptions in 2026?**

**A:** The Arranged Employment Opinion (AEO) was a pre-2013 mechanism used by employers to support a foreign worker's Federal Skilled Worker permanent residence application by attesting to a genuine, permanent job offer, without requiring a full LMIA labour market test. It was **eliminated on May 4, 2013**, and replaced by the LMIA process. Today, the term "arranged employment" refers generically to a valid job offer that can earn CRS points in Express Entry — these points were **removed for most occupations in 2026**, now only available for TEER 0 senior management roles (50 points). "GEO" (Global Employment Opinion) is not a current IRCC term; practitioners may use it informally to refer to an LMIA-exempt opinion under certain trade agreements, but the correct official mechanisms are LMIA (for TFWP) and LMIA exemption codes (for IMP). In 2026, for tech workers, **ICT and C10 are the relevant LMIA-exempt pathways** — neither AEO nor GEO are active frameworks in the current system.

## Key Takeaways

1. **ICT (C61/C62/C63) is the most reliable LMIA-exempt pathway for tech talent at multinationals** — structured criteria, predictable timelines (4–12 weeks overseas, same-day at POE for visa-exempt), and a clear runway to PR via Express Entry/PNP. The "specialized knowledge" trap (C63) is the biggest risk; expect high scrutiny on proving knowledge is proprietary and not widely available in Canada.

2. **C10 Significant Benefit became dramatically narrower on February 24, 2026** — reserved for "unique or exceptional situations" with quantifiable evidence of broad community/regional impact. It is no longer a flexible LMIA alternative for routine tech hires. Refusal risk is high (estimated 20-35%+, now likely higher). Only pursue with strong, third-party-verified evidence of benefit extending beyond the employer.

3. **The removal of arranged employment CRS points in Express Entry (2025/2026) reshaped the PR strategy** — ICT and C10 holders can no longer rely on 50–200 job offer points. The new path: PNP nomination (600 points), category-based draws (STEM/French at lower CRS), or maximizing language/education scores. Plan your PR transition from Day 1 of your work permit.

4. **The Start-Up Visa program is paused** (Jan 1, 2026) with a new entrepreneur pilot expected. For founders, C11 owner-operator or provincial entrepreneur streams are the interim alternatives. Processing times for existing SUV queue are 3.3–4.3 years.

5. **Employer compliance obligations are real** — Even without an LMIA, the CAD $230 employer compliance fee, 6-year record-keeping requirement, and potential compliance reviews apply to all IMP exemptions (ICT, C10, C16, CUSMA). Non-compliance can bar the employer from the program for 1–2 years.

## FAQ Items

### 1. Can I apply for permanent residence directly through an ICT work permit?

No — the ICT is a temporary work permit, not a permanent residence program. However, the skilled Canadian work experience gained (TEER 0 or 1) makes you eligible for Express Entry (CEC after 12 months) and Provincial Nominee Programs. Many ICT holders achieve PR within 2–4 years of arriving in Canada. The key change in 2025/2026: you can no longer claim 50–200 CRS points for arranged employment (job offer) in Express Entry, so PNP or category-based draws are now essential.

### 2. What is the minimum salary I must be paid on an ICT work permit?

For **specialized knowledge workers (C63)**, IRCC requires the salary to meet or exceed the **prevailing Canadian wage** for the occupation (NOC-specific). For executives and senior managers (C62), the wage must be defensible as commensurate with the seniority of the role. If the salary is significantly below the Canadian market rate, it triggers additional scrutiny and potential refusal. There is no statutory minimum for ICT, but the wage must be consistent with the employer's stated duties and the worker's qualifications.

### 3. Does my spouse get an open work permit if I'm on an ICT or C10 permit?

It depends. **Spousal open work permits (C41)** were tightened on **January 21, 2025**. Eligibility now requires the principal worker to be in a **TEER 0 or 1 occupation** (or eligible TEER 2/3) and have **at least 16 months remaining** on their work permit at the time of the spouse's application. If your initial ICT permit is 3 years, the spouse can apply immediately. If it's a 1-year new-office ICT (C61), the spouse may not meet the 16-month threshold. Dependent children are **no longer eligible** for open work permits under the spousal measures.

### 4. Can I use the Global Skills Strategy (GSS) for faster processing on an ICT or C10 application?

Yes. The GSS offers **2-week processing** for employer-specific work permits in TEER 0 or 1 occupations. For **LMIA-exempt** workers (ICT, C10, C16), the application is automatically flagged for GSS if the role meets the TEER criteria — no special questions need to be answered. For **LMIA-required** roles, the employer must use the Global Talent Stream (expedited LMIA, ~2 weeks) and the worker must answer "yes" to the GTS-specific question. GSS is available for both streams.

### 5. What happens if my ICT work permit expires and I haven't gotten PR yet?

You have options depending on your timeline and maximum stay limits:
- **Apply for an extension** (if within the 5/7-year cumulative limit) — process online from inside Canada, 8–16 weeks
- **Switch categories** — after exhausting ICT maximum stay (7 yrs exec/manager, 5 yrs specialized knowledge), you must leave Canada for ≥1 year (cooling-off). During that year, you could return on an LMIA-based permit, CUSMA professional, or wait and reapply for ICT
- **Apply for PR** — if you have ≥12 months Canadian experience and meet Express Entry/PNP criteria, you can apply for PR and a **Bridging Open Work Permit (BOWP)** to maintain status while PR is processed
- **Best practice:** Start your PR application 12–18 months before hitting your ICT maximum stay ceiling.
